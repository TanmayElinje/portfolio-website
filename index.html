The Complete Tanmay Story

From Ship Engines to Data Engines

My journey began in the world of marine engineering - not because of some childhood dream about the sea, but simply because it felt way more interesting than your typical engineering degree. Good money, free world travel, and challenging work? Sign me up! I completed my B.Tech in Marine Engineering from Marine Engineering and Research Institute, Mumbai (now Indian Maritime University), where I spent four years learning to work with everything from main engines to fuel purifiers, freshwater generators, and boilers.

I spent time sailing the waters, working with all kinds of machinery in conditions that would make most people seasick just thinking about them. Think extreme working hours, immense stress, and machinery that absolutely cannot fail when you're in the middle of the ocean. It was exactly as challenging and exciting as I'd hoped - until medical reasons forced me to leave this industry.

The Data Science Discovery

There wasn't some dramatic "aha moment" where I suddenly saw the light of data science. Instead, a friend who'd also left the industry told me about a course he pursued named PG-Diploma in Big Data Analytics from CDAC. After doing my own research, I thought, "Hey, this looks pretty interesting too!" Sometimes the best career changes happen organically.

Six months ago, I enrolled in the same program. Fast forward to a month ago - I graduated with 3rd rank in my class! Not bad for someone who discovered this field just a year ago through YouTube videos, right?

My Technical Arsenal

I'm now proficient in Python, MySQL, PySpark, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Power BI and AWS. Currently, I'm knee-deep in building projects to showcase on my resume while simultaneously binge-watching Stanford University's machine learning videos on YouTube (because apparently, I like my learning with a side of academic rigor).

Life Beyond Data

I'm a complete gym freak. Working out and running aren't just hobbies - they're basically my second religion. I also love football and volleyball so much that I represented my school in both sports and was the football team captain for two years. These days, you'll also find me watching anime or plotting my next chess move.

My Superpowers

When I commit to something, I go all in. Day and night, 200% effort - that's just how I operate. Leadership roles have always found their way to me naturally: school football captain, house captain, college fest event coordinator, core committee member, and most recently, technical lead and project lead for my diploma team.

Adaptability is my secret weapon. After working on ships under extreme conditions, harsh weather, and impossible working hours, I can honestly say I can adapt to pretty much any situation. Plus, I've developed this superpower of staying calm under pressure - which turns out to be incredibly useful when your code decides to break at 2 AM.

Patience is another one of my key strengths - which is needed even more so when your ML model has been running for hours only to end with a negative r-squared score. Trust me, those moments test your soul, but I've learned that the best insights often come after the most frustrating failures!

My Kryptonite

I have this stubborn habit of not asking for help easily. I'll spend hours trying to solve something myself when a five-minute conversation with a colleague could save me half a day. I'm actively working on this (seriously, I've started asking my teachers and colleagues for help when I'm stuck - character development!).

Also, here's something funny: I can remember exactly what someone said to me five years ago, but ask me what you told me yesterday? Complete blank. My brain apparently operates on some weird long-term storage system.

My Philosophy

"Just trying to be a better version of myself than yesterday." The data science and IT field in general is massive, and discovering something new every day is genuinely exciting. But here's the thing - motivation can only get you so far. It's discipline and hard work that matter in the long run.

People ask me how I stay motivated to go to the gym every day. Truth is, I don't need motivation anymore - it's become a habit I love so much that I feel incomplete without it. That same mindset is what I'm bringing to my data science journey.

Future Ambitions

My long-term goal: Become a data scientist who can transform complex business problems into elegant, data-driven solutions. I am excited to apply my unique background to new challenges, whether in maritime analytics, sports data science, or another emerging field.

Having successfully transitioned from engineering physical engines to data engines, I am confident and excited for the journey ahead.
